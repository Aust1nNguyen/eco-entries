Supply and demand form the most fundamental concepts of economics. Demand and supply represent the relationship between the quantity of a commodity that producers wish to sell and the quantity that consumers wish to buy.

ELASTICITY

The law of demand states that a fall in the price of a good raises the quantity demanded. The price elasticity of demand measures how much the quantity demanded responds to a change in price.

CONSUMER AND PRODUCER SURPLUS

Economic surplus refers to two related quantities: consumer surplus and producer surplus. Consumer surplus is the difference between what a consumer is willing to pay and what they actually pay for a good or service. The producer surplus is the difference between the actual price of a good or service–the market price–and the lowest price a producer would be willing to accept for a good.

In this topic we will learn the difference between consumper and surplus and what this means for market efficiency. Click here to learn more!
