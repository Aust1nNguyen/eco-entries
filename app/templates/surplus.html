Definition of Consumer Surplus

This is the difference between what the consumer pays and what he would have been willing to pay. For example: If you would be willing to pay £50 for a ticket to see the F. A. Cup final, but you can buy a ticket for £40. In this case, your consumer surplus is £10.

Definition of producer surplus

This is the difference between the price a firm receives and the price it would be willing to sell it at. If a firm would sell a good at £4, but the market price is £7, the producer surplus is £3.
